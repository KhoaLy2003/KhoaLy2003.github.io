## Going deep on unfamiliar ground

Most of my recent work has been in familiar territory. This week flipped that. My current assignment pushed me into a stack I hadn't spent serious time with: **Azure Cloud, Terraform, and monitoring infrastructure**. Not a quick skim — a real deep dive.

There's a specific kind of discomfort that comes with picking up tools — <Icon name="logos:terraform" /> Terraform, <Icon name="logos:azure-icon" /> Azure — you don't yet have an intuition for. You read the docs, run into walls, backtrack, and slowly build a mental model that actually holds. That's where I spent most of my working hours this week.

What made it worth it: watching the pieces connect. Provisioning infrastructure with Terraform, seeing how it maps to Azure resources, understanding how monitoring fits into the whole picture — it's one of those areas where the more you learn, the more the scope of what you *don't* know becomes visible. Humbling, but motivating.

I'll write more specifically about what I learned once I've had time to sit with it. For now: Azure and Terraform are solidly on my radar.

## Welcoming newcomers at KMS Technology

Outside of the deep technical work, I got to participate in an **Orientation Sharing session** for new joiners at KMS Technology. It's easy to underestimate how much these sessions matter — not just for the people coming in, but for whoever's sharing.

Explaining your work, your team's practices, and what the company's culture actually feels like forces you to articulate things you usually leave implicit. I came away with a clearer sense of what I'd want a new teammate to know on day one, and what we take for granted that we probably shouldn't.

If you ever get the chance to be part of onboarding sessions, take it. It's low-key one of the better ways to reflect on your own experience.
